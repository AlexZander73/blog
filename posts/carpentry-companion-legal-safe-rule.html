Carpentry Companion: The Legal-Safe Rule That Shaped Everything

This Carpentry Companion entry covers a non-code decision that changed the full product direction. I locked the content model to legal-safe references early, and that constraint still guides every feature.

February 21, 20264 min read

Notes Systems Apps

The rule

I decided that Carpentry Companion would never copy NCC or Standards text into the app. Instead, each reference entry would use an identifier, a link, and a plain-language user-authored summary.

At first this felt like giving up convenience. Full text in-app would have been easier for some users in the short term. But I wanted a product that could scale without legal gray zones.

Why this mattered emotionally

I felt a lot of tension here. I wanted to help users fast, and I also wanted to build responsibly. Choosing the safer model felt slower on day one, but it removed a constant background anxiety from every publishing decision.

That peace of mind mattered. It let me focus on the actual user value: workflows, calculators, and clean guidance, not risky content shortcuts.

Lesson and next move

Lesson: constraints can make the product clearer, not weaker. The legal-safe model forced better writing and clearer links.

Next step after setting this rule was to strengthen the guided calculator flow, so users still got practical help without needing embedded standards text.

Next move: expand guidance depth while keeping legal boundaries strict.
