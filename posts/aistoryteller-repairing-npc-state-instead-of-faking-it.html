AIStoryTeller: Repairing NPC State Instead of Faking It

The 2026-02-19 fix commit is one of the most honest moments in this repo. It says the NPC pipeline needed a repair, not a cosmetic patch.

February 19, 20262 min read

AI Systems Notes Build Logs

Honesty beats illusion

One of the fastest ways to lose trust in an AI product is to let the UI pretend it knows more than the system actually knows. That is why the 2026-02-19 commit matters to me. It directly fixed NPC list handling, added a raw toggle, and worked on status fallback. Even without reading a journal, I can tell what kind of problem this was. Story output and UI state had drifted apart enough that the app needed a way to show the truth more directly.

That raw toggle is the detail I keep coming back to. Whenever a product adds a raw view, it is usually admitting that the polished view cannot always be trusted yet. I respect that admission. In an AI storytelling app, derived state is always tempting. But if the parser is brittle, the polished layer becomes a liar. A raw escape hatch is not glamorous. It is responsible.

Repairing trust

The emotional moment here is frustration, but it is the useful kind. I dislike the class of bug that makes a system feel half-alive, where some of the right names and statuses appear just often enough to look convincing. Those bugs waste time because they do not fail loudly. The concrete change was to repair NPC list behavior and add status fallback instead of doubling down on inference.

What I learned

The lesson is one I trust more every year: canonical state beats decorative certainty. If the UI depends on extracted story details, it needs a fallback that is less clever and more dependable. It also needs tooling that helps me see what the system actually received before it tries to summarize it.

This commit is small compared with the giant feature drops in the repo, but it may be more important. It shows the point where AIStoryTeller started choosing honesty over illusion in one of its most fragile paths.

Next move: keep moving story-derived data toward structured truth and treat prose parsing as assistive, not authoritative.
