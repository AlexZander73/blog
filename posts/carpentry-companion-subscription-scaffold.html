Backlog Entry: Subscription Gating Entered the App

Backlog entry for 2026-02-25. This was the moment the app crossed a line: from useful prototype to something that needed release structure and pricing logic.

February 25, 20264 min read

Apps Launches Systems

What changed

I added the first real App Store scaffolding and subscription gate flow. I also pushed quick calculator routes and wizard improvements so the paid structure was attached to real value, not just locked screens.

This was also near the period where formula-route bugs and NaN behavior surfaced. It was a reminder that monetization only works if core trust stays intact.

How it felt

I felt exposed. Pricing and access decisions make a project feel public very fast, even before launch. It forced me to ask better questions: what stays free, what earns its place behind a paywall, and where frustration starts.

The emotional win was clarity. Once the free-to-paid boundary was visible, roadmap decisions got easier.

Lesson and next move

Lesson: business model choices are product design choices. They change architecture, UX, and trust.

Next move was to package workflows better, so users could see clear paths through the app rather than a flat list of tools.

Next move: make package structure obvious so users can move faster.
