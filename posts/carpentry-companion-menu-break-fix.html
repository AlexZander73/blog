Backlog Entry: The Day the Menu Broke on iPhone

Backlog entry for 2026-03-09. A major iPhone menu regression landed at the worst time. It looked small in code, but it broke core navigation on real devices.

March 9, 20264 min read

Build Logs Design Apps

How it failed

On iPhone, the burger menu became transparent, stacked over live content, and sometimes locked scrolling. Users could not trust where they were in the app. That is a serious failure for a tool meant for quick decisions.

I moved menu placement out of risky stacking contexts, fixed body scroll locking, and adjusted sheet sizing and layering rules. It took multiple commits because each fix uncovered another edge case.

How it felt

I felt defensive at first, then focused. Seeing a broken menu screenshot from real use cuts through ego quickly. It reminded me to test interaction layers on device before calling UI work done.

This was also a morale test. Repetitive bug-fix loops are tiring, but finishing them creates real confidence.

Lesson and next move

Lesson: if navigation is unstable, everything feels unstable.

Next move was to pair this nav stability pass with a visual system cleanup, so the app felt coherent after the technical fixes.

Next move: keep the UX calm and consistent after recovery work.
