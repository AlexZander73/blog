Carpentry Companion: Day Zero and the First Job Mode Build

This is a Carpentry Companion entry written after the fact, but the date is real. 2026-02-20 is when Carpentry Companion shifted from idea energy into something that could actually help on a work site.

February 20, 20264 min read

Apps Build Logs Tools

What changed on 2026-02-20

I shipped the first serious shape of the app: Job Mode, an audit trail, export paths, and stronger media support for formulas and lessons. Before that, this project felt like a simple utility. After this day, it felt like a workflow tool.

I also expanded the tools section with filtering and credits. It was not glamorous work, but it made the app feel honest and usable. The point was simple: a tradie should open this and get useful answers quickly, even with poor signal on site.

How it felt

I felt two things at once: proud of the momentum and worried about scope. Adding audit logs and exports made the project heavier overnight. But leaving those out would have made it less trustworthy in real handover moments.

The emotional shift was important. I stopped asking, "Does this look like an app?" and started asking, "Would I trust this under time pressure?" That question changed every decision after this point.

Lesson and next move

Lesson: utility beats cleverness. When a tool helps a real task flow from start to finish, people forgive rough edges.

Next move on my board after this commit day was to tighten formulas and expand guided flows, so the app could support both study sessions and site work without forcing users to bounce between tools.

Next move: keep the workflow spine strong while adding guided calculators.
