Backlog Entry: Choosing Native Without Rewriting

Backlog entry for 2026-02-28. This was architecture decision day: keep one codebase and wrap for native with Capacitor instead of splitting focus across separate rewrites.

February 28, 20264 min read

Apps Systems Experiments

Why I made this call

Real usage kept pointing to iPhone and iPad contexts. People wanted this in hand on site, not buried in a browser tab. Capacitor let me keep momentum and avoid a full rewrite panic.

I added native projects and billing environment templates so release prep was part of the repo, not a side document.

The emotional tradeoff

I felt both relief and pressure. Relief because the path became clear. Pressure because native release work introduces admin overhead, metadata, screenshots, and tooling friction that code-only builders often underestimate.

Still, clarity beats comfort. One focused channel is easier to ship than two half-supported channels.

Lesson and next move

Lesson: architecture should follow user context, not developer habit.

Next move after adding native scaffolding was direct device hardening: safe areas, startup behavior, and media rendering under real iOS conditions.

Next move: run hard iPhone tests and fix whatever breaks first.
