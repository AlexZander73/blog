Carpentry Companion: Making the UI Feel Alive, Not Loud

Second Carpentry Companion entry for 2026-03-09. After navigation fixes, I tackled a softer issue: the app worked, but it did not feel clear or alive enough to build trust quickly.

March 9, 20264 min read

Design Apps Notes

What changed in the design pass

I introduced a more consistent accent system on key cards and headlines while keeping dense information cards neutral. The goal was energy at the right moments, not gradients everywhere.

I also renamed Work Packs to Plans. That one word made the product feel less mechanical and easier to explain. Users understand a plan faster than a pack.

The human side of design work

I used to treat design polish as a final pass. This week changed that. When the interface looked flat, people assumed the app was less mature than it was.

Emotionally, this was a confidence recovery moment. A calmer visual language made the whole product feel more intentional.

Lesson and next move

Lesson: visual coherence is part of usability, especially for practical tools used under time pressure.

Next step was to lock channel strategy and remove tooling that no longer matched how the app would ship.

Next move: finalize distribution direction and simplify release operations.
