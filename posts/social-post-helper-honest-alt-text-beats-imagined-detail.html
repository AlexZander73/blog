Social Post Helper: Honest Alt Text Beats Imagined Detail

A non-AI media tool can be helpful without pretending it understands the image. I wanted the writing to stay honest enough that users could trust what the app was doing and what it was not doing.

March 12, 20262 min read

AI Design Notes

Refusing fake understanding

One of the quietest but most important decisions in Social Post Helper was how to handle alt text. This app does not do scene understanding. It does not inspect the image content the way a vision model would. That creates a temptation: write something polished anyway and hope it sounds useful. I felt that temptation because richer text always looks better in a demo. I also knew it would be a mistake.

Constraining the generator on purpose

The concrete change was building the draft generator so alt text stays anchored to known facts only. Dates, location metadata, category, and the user's own notes are fair game. Invented descriptions are not. The test suite makes that stance explicit by checking that the output does not pretend to infer visual scene details. I care about that more than I care about making the copy sound clever.

This matters because accessibility text is not the place for fake confidence. If the app cannot actually know what is in the image, dressing up a guess as a description is not helpful. It is misleading. The same principle applies more broadly to the whole product. Social Post Helper is useful because it reduces repetitive work around structure, not because it secretly knows more than it does.

Restraint as product quality

The lesson here was that restraint is a form of product quality. It is easy to confuse polish with trust. For this app, trust is the more valuable asset. When a user sees a plain but honest line, they know where the machine stops and where their judgment starts. That boundary matters, especially in a tool designed around deliberate non-AI constraints.

There is still room to make the experience better. Honest does not have to mean stiff. The next versions can help users add richer manual context for alt text without crossing into invented description.

Next move: add stronger manual guidance for accessibility text before considering any later image-understanding upgrade.
