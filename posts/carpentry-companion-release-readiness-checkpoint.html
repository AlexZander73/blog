Release Readiness Checkpoint: Where Carpentry Companion Stands Today

Checkpoint entry dated 2026-03-10. Not a launch post. This is a clear status report from pre-launch: what is solid, what is still blocked, and what I need to finish next.

March 10, 20265 min read

Launches Build Logs Apps

What is confirmed as of 2026-03-10

The repo now has 34 commits since starting on 2026-02-20. The app includes 18 formulas, 6 lessons, 108 tools, references, plans, and job workflows with export and audit history.

Automated tests passed 9/9 and native prep build plus sync completed successfully on this date. I also added a privacy policy document to satisfy store metadata requirements.

Where friction still exists

The hard blockers are still release operations: final App Store metadata links, build selection state, IAP state alignment, and full purchase-restore validation in TestFlight.

Emotionally, this is the part that drains me most. It is less creative than building features, but it decides whether the app actually ships.

Lesson and next move

Lesson: release readiness is evidence, not vibe. Passing tests, traceable commits, and clean checklists matter more than saying "almost done."

I am treating this as a controlled final stretch, one step at a time: update build, finalize metadata, submit with IAPs, verify purchase and restore, then communicate launch clearly.

Next move: submit the version and close the last operational blockers to reach public launch.
